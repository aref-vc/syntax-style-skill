Syntax Style

Sentence-level craft, on demand.
Distilled from Virginia Tufte, Artful Sentences: Syntax as Style (Graphics Press, 2006).
The model: a base clause plus free modifiers at the front, middle, or end. Same model, two words or sixty.   The engine failed → The engine failed, a cough then silence, the boat drifting toward the rocks.   Right-branching cumulative is the workhorse; the artful version is often longer. Monotone (all short) and fog (nested clauses) flank it.

One transformation

There was a reduction in costs as a result of the team's optimization of the pipeline.
→ The team optimized the pipeline and costs fell.
It's not just a tool, it's a platform that transforms your workflow.
→ The tool became a platform.

Three rules that override the rest

Repetition is a tool. Deliberate repetition builds rhythm. Kill accidental sameness, keep the chosen move.
Variation cuts both ways. All-short is monotone too. Set a long sentence next to a short one.
Shorter isn't better. Fix a bad long sentence with a clean base + free modifiers. Prefer the pair to the triad.
