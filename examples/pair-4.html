Elaborate · Marketing · Cumulative with an absolute
Before · default
Our app tracks your habits. It sends you reminders. It shows your progress. It keeps you motivated over time.
→
After · crafted
The app tracks your habits and nudges you when you slip, a quiet mirror held up each morning, its streak counter ticking, until the habit no longer needs the app at all.
The move: one base clause carries an appositive (“a quiet mirror”) and a nominative absolute (“its streak counter ticking”), then lands the payoff. Four flat sentences could not build this arc.
